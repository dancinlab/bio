## Why it matters — why knee cartilage is uniquely hard

Senescent-cell cleanup ([[SENOLYX-AG]]) opens the regeneration gate on its own in tissues like **gums · hair · retina**.
Those tissues have **blood vessels**, so blood flow delivers both the drug and the raw material to rebuild cells.

But **knee cartilage has no blood vessels (avascular · acellular).** So:

```
House (tissue) repair analogy
───────────────
Vascular tissue = a neighborhood with open roads   → one cleanup truck finishes the job
Knee cartilage  = a cut-off remote island          → the cleanup truck can't even get in (δ),
                                                      and no workers or materials arrive (A)
                                                      → cleanup alone can never fix it
```

What we **proved mathematically**: senescent-cell cleanup alone (with any delivery you like) means the knee-cartilage healing gate
**fundamentally never closes** (ceiling 0.755 < required 0.90). Of the four therapies, knee cartilage is the only exception.

## What we found — "not one gate, but three"

```
Before (prior approach)             After (our finding)
─────────────────                   ─────────────────
 ▢ Cleanup alone heals it        →  ▢ Knee cartilage is a triple gate
   (cleanup only)                     δ penetration × φ cleanup × A regeneration, at once
 ▢ Develop a strong-binding      →  ▢ Existing protein regen drugs
   regen drug (invent a new drug)     penetrate cartilage at δ≈0 (surface only)
 ▢ Build everything new          →  ▢ 3 layers are ours + 1 is
                                      an existing best-in-class part (kartogenin)
```

Key counterexample: a powerful regeneration protein like **sprifermin (FGF-18)** has top-tier strength, but **it is too bulky to break through the cartilage mesh (δ≈0).** A textbook counterexample of "strong ≠ arrives."


| Part | Role | Ours / off-the-shelf | Analogy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cationic carrier | Delivers drug deep into cartilage (δ) | **our physics** | a bridge laid to the cut-off island |
| SENOLYX-AG cleanup | Removes senescent cells (φ) | **ours** | the cleanup truck that clears the spot |
| Progenitor migration | Draws cells into the empty spot (A_endo) | **ours** | workers walking in |
| **Kartogenin** | Differentiates those cells into real cartilage (A) | **best off-the-shelf** | materials + blueprint |

- **Why kartogenin?** Regeneration proteins (sprifermin etc.) penetrate cartilage at δ≈0 (surface only). Kartogenin is a small molecule, so loaded onto the cationic
  carrier it goes deep into the cartilage — the only off-the-shelf part that satisfies **both** strength (A) and penetration (δ).
