## Why it matters — the real weakness of existing numbing creams

Topical numbing creams (EMLA, etc.) already exist. But the real problem is **not "does it work fast?" — it's safety and coverage**.
- Using EMLA on children carries a risk of **methemoglobinemia** (a side effect where the blood can't carry oxygen) — from the prilocaine ingredient.
- Every procedure needs a different product and concentration, and over-the-counter (OTC) and prescription (Rx) products are all mixed together.

NUMB is **not "faster numbing"** — it takes direct aim at the weaknesses above.
