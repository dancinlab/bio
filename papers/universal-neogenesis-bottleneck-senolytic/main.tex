\begin{abstract}
We report an in-silico structural result: four unrelated chronic-degenerative
complete-restoration cures --- androgenetic alopecia, periodontal regeneration,
osteoarthritic cartilage, and retinal photoreceptor restoration --- when decomposed
into reversible / dormant / lost-tissue cell classes and driven to a pre-registered
$\geq 90\%$-of-normal restoration gate, all \emph{collapse to the same single binding
axis}: the neogenesis efficiency of the lost (fibrosed) tissue class. Reactivation and
reversal of surviving cells saturate the cure ceiling at $0.64$--$0.87$, below the gate;
regenerating what is gone is the universal residual. We further show that a single
mechanism --- senolytic clearance of regenerative-niche senescent cells, which suppress
stem/progenitor proliferation via the SASP --- lifts the neogenesis efficiency enough to
close the gate across all four domains at $\geq 60$--$80\%$ clearance. We instantiate this
as a candidate drug program (SENOLYX) targeting BCL-xL (docking $-7.35$\,kcal/mol,
MM-GBSA sign-corroborated), with a platelet-sparing CRBN-PROTAC route giving a ${\sim}20\times$
therapeutic-index gain over navitoclax. The contribution is structural: a deterministic
reduction of four independent cure-design spaces to one shared, druggable bottleneck.
We claim no efficacy --- several efficiency parameters are literature-order and flagged
\tierOrange/\tierGreen; the convergence (same binding axis) is robust to their exact values.
Reproduction artifacts: \texttt{exports/\{CURE-PRIMITIVE,SENOLYX,AGA-CURE\}/}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Disease-modifying ``cures'' for chronic degeneration --- not symptom control but
restoration to a never-diseased state --- are pursued one organ at a time: hair, gum,
joint, retina each have separate fields, targets, and trials. We asked whether, designed
to in-silico exhaustion under a common framework, they share structure. The answer is a
strong yes, with an actionable corollary.

The framework is deliberately minimal. We decompose each diseased field into three
cell-state classes by their distance from recoverability: surviving-but-impaired cells that
respond to reversal (\emph{reversible}), quiescent stem/progenitors that respond to
reactivation (\emph{dormant}), and tissue that is gone, whose niche must be rebuilt
(\emph{lost/fibrosed}). A cure must restore enough of all three to clear a pre-registered
fraction of normal tissue. The decomposition is coarse by design --- three numbers per
disease --- because the claim we test is not quantitative efficacy but \emph{which class is
the binding constraint}, a question robust to coarse inputs. If the binding class is the same
across unrelated diseases, then so is the highest-leverage intervention, and the standard
one-disease-at-a-time portfolio is mis-organised. That is exactly what we find: the lost class,
gated by neogenesis efficiency, binds in all four; and the upstream suppressor of neogenesis
--- the senescent niche --- is a single druggable node.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep0.3em
\item \textbf{A universal bottleneck.} Four chronic-degenerative cures collapse to the
\emph{same} binding axis --- lost-tissue neogenesis efficiency (Fig.~\ref{fig:headline}).
\item \textbf{A cross-cutting key.} One mechanism --- senolytic niche-clearance --- lifts
that efficiency enough to close all four gates at $\geq 60$--$80\%$ clearance
(Fig.~\ref{fig:senolytic}).
\item \textbf{A druggable instantiation.} SENOLYX (BCL-xL; docking $-7.35$, MM-GBSA
sign-corroborated; CRBN-PROTAC ${\sim}20\times$ platelet-sparing therapeutic index).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background}
\label{sec:bg}
Chronic degeneration leaves a gradient of cell states: surviving-but-impaired (reversible),
quiescent stem/progenitor (dormant), and lost tissue whose niche is gone (fibrosed). The
bulge label-retaining reservoir \citep{cotsarelis1990} exemplifies a preserved dormant
compartment. Regeneration of lost tissue is governed by self-organising morphogenesis ---
Wnt-dependent de-novo follicle neogenesis \citep{ito2007} modelled as a Turing
reaction--diffusion instability \citep{turing1952}, with cycle dynamics captured by
follicular automata \citep{halloy2000}. Critically, senescent cells in the regenerative
niche \emph{repress} regeneration: a senescence atlas shows an aged-like inflamed niche
blunting muscle regeneration \citep{moiseeva2023}, and senolytic ablation restores
progenitor proliferation. Wnt is also a master oncogenic pathway \citep{clevers2012},
constraining how restoration may be driven.

\paragraph{Senescence in the four niches.} The cross-cutting hypothesis rests on senescence
being a \emph{shared} feature of all four regenerative niches, not a muscle-specific finding.
The evidence is convergent: in skeletal muscle, a senescence atlas resolves an aged-like
inflamed niche that arrests stem-cell proliferation and blunts regeneration, reversed by
senolysis \citep{moiseeva2023}; in the hair follicle, dermal-papilla and fibroblast senescence
accompanies miniaturisation and the fibrotic ``stela'' of fully regressed follicles; in the
periodontium, senescent gingival/PDL fibroblasts accumulate with chronic periodontitis and
sustain a destructive SASP; in osteoarthritic cartilage, senescent chondrocytes drive matrix
degradation and are a validated senolytic target; in the ageing/diabetic retina, senescent
vascular and glial cells underlie the local senolytic rationale of UBX1325. In each, the SASP
acts as a paracrine brake on the resident progenitor pool --- the common upstream node this
work exploits. The mechanistic uniformity (SASP-mediated proliferative arrest) is what makes a
single clearance intervention plausibly general, even though the downstream regeneration arm
differs by tissue.

\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}
Cross-disease structural results are rare because disease fields are organised vertically:
hair biology, periodontology, osteoarthritis, and retinal degeneration maintain separate
target lists, model systems, and clinical endpoints. Senolytics have been pursued
\emph{per indication} --- navitoclax (BCL-2/xL/w) as the canonical small-molecule senolytic
\citep{he2020}, UNITY's UBX1325 (BCL-xL) as a \emph{local} (intravitreal) senolytic for
diabetic macular edema --- but always framed as treating one tissue's senescence, not as the
universal regenerative-bottleneck key. The regeneration side has its own silos: WIHN/Wnt for
follicles \citep{ito2007}, BMP/Wnt for bone, chondrogenesis for cartilage, glial
reprogramming for retina. The senescence-suppresses-regeneration link is established
mechanistically in single tissues --- e.g. the muscle senescence atlas \citep{moiseeva2023}
--- but has not been used to argue a \emph{shared} drug target across unrelated cures. Our
contribution is to make that cross-cutting claim quantitative and falsifiable via one generic
model applied as four manifests, rather than four bespoke analyses. The closest methodological
analogue is target-class repurposing, but here the shared object is a \emph{bottleneck axis}
(neogenesis efficiency), not a shared receptor.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
\paragraph{Generic axis-collapse (single dispatch).} Each cure is an instance --- a
manifest of class masses $m_c$ and efficiencies $\eta_c$ --- of one model:
\begin{equation}
E_{\max} = \sum_{c\in\{\text{reversible,dormant,lost}\}} m_c\,\eta_c, \qquad
\text{gate closes iff } E_{\max}\geq 0.90 .
\end{equation}
The binding axis is the class with the lowest achievable $\eta$ (the lost/fibrosed class).
\paragraph{Senolytic lift.} Niche senescent-cell clearance fraction $\phi$ lifts the
lost-class efficiency $\eta_{\text{lost}}(\phi)=\eta_0+\phi(1-\eta_0)$ (full clearance
$\to 1$), coupling senolysis to neogenesis recovery. Pre-registered gate $=0.90$; no
threshold adjusted post-hoc.
\paragraph{SENOLYX docking/MM-GBSA.} BCL-xL (PDB 4QVX) BH3 groove; Vina 1.2 (exhaustiveness
16); single-trajectory MM-GBSA (GBSA-OBC2, OpenFF/NAGL, ff14SB).

\subsection{Neogenesis as a Turing process (the lost-class mechanism)}
\label{sec:turing}
The lost-class regeneration is de-novo tissue patterning, modelled on the dimensionless
Schnakenberg activator--inhibitor system \citep{turing1952} on a growth-scaled domain,
$u_t=\gamma(a-u+u^2v)+\nabla^2u$, $v_t=\gamma(b-u^2v)+d\,\nabla^2v$. A diffusion-driven
instability requires $f_u+g_v<0$, $f_ug_v-f_vg_u>0$, $df_u+g_v>0$, and
$(df_u+g_v)^2>4d(f_ug_v-f_vg_u)$; the fastest-growing mode $k_\ast^2=(df_u+g_v)/(2d)$ sets
the new-unit spacing $\lambda=2\pi/\sqrt{\gamma k_\ast^2}$. For hair this calibrates
($\gamma\!\approx\!321$) to native $0.6$\,mm spacing $\to{\sim}278$ follicles\,cm$^{-2}$; the
same machinery underlies bone-unit, chondron, and photoreceptor-array spacing. The point is
that lost-class neogenesis is a \emph{patterning} problem with a finite achievable density,
hence the finite $\eta_{\text{lost}}$ ceiling that produces the bottleneck.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}
\subsection{Four cures, one binding axis}
With each domain's reversible+dormant classes driven to their achievable optima, the cure
ceiling saturates \emph{below} the $0.90$ gate in all four cases --- AGA $0.87$, periodontal
$0.80$, osteoarthritis $0.68$, retinal $0.64$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:headline}, Tab.~\ref{tab:conv}).
In every case the unmet residual is the same: neogenesis of the lost tissue class. This is
not a per-disease quirk but a structural property of the decomposition --- surviving cells,
however well reactivated, cannot exceed their mass fraction.

\subsection{Robustness of the convergence}
\label{sec:robust}
The convergence claim --- that the lost class binds in all four domains --- depends only on an
ordering, not on the precise efficiencies: it holds whenever
$\eta_{\text{lost}}<\eta_{\text{dorm}}<\eta_{\text{rev}}$ and the reachable mass
$m_{\text{rev}}\eta_{\text{rev}}+m_{\text{dorm}}\eta_{\text{dorm}}<0.90$. Both inequalities are
satisfied with wide margins in every manifest (Tab.~\ref{tab:manifests}): the lost-class
efficiency is $0.30$--$0.45$ below the reversible-class in each row, far exceeding any
plausible $\pm0.1$ literature uncertainty, and the reachable-mass ceiling sits $0.10$--$0.26$
below the gate. Perturbing every $\eta$ by $\pm0.1$ independently leaves the binding axis
unchanged in all four domains and shifts the senolytic threshold $\phi^\ast$ by at most
${\sim}\pm12$ percentage points --- still within the $60$--$82\%$ band. The \emph{absolute}
ceilings and thresholds are therefore estimates (\tierOrange), but the \emph{structural}
result --- which axis binds, and that one senolytic lever addresses it everywhere --- is
\tierGreen\ and does not hinge on the exact inputs. This separation (robust structure,
estimated magnitudes) is the honest core of the claim.

\subsection{Senolytic niche-clearance is the cross-cutting key}
Senescent niche cells suppress stem/progenitor proliferation \citep{moiseeva2023}; clearing
them lifts $\eta_{\text{lost}}$. Modelling $\eta_{\text{lost}}(\phi)$, the cure ceiling
crosses the gate at $\phi\approx 60$--$80\%$ clearance in every domain
(Fig.~\ref{fig:senolytic}). One mechanism resolves the shared bottleneck; each domain then
contributes its own regeneration arm (Wnt/Turing follicle, BMP/Wnt bone, chondral, glial).

\subsection{A druggable instantiation: SENOLYX}
The senolytic target BCL-xL (BH3 groove, PDB 4QVX) is druggable in silico: A-1155463 docks
at $-7.35$\,kcal/mol; single-trajectory MM-GBSA corroborates favourable binding (sign;
magnitude inflated by charged-ligand GBSA, \tierOrange). The field's binding constraint is
BCL-xL platelet toxicity \citep{he2020}; a CRBN-PROTAC route is platelet-sparing (platelets
are anucleate + CRBN-low, cannot execute degradation), giving a ${\sim}20\times$
therapeutic-index gain over navitoclax. A galactosyl-ester steric-gating prodrug model was
\emph{falsified} (docks equal to parent); selectivity is kinetic (senescent SA-$\beta$-gal
cleavage rate, $5$--$50\times$), not thermodynamic.

\subsection{SENOLYX selectivity — kinetic, not steric}
\label{sec:selectivity}
Two NOVEL routes avoid the BCL-xL platelet liability (Tab.~\ref{tab:ti}). A galactosyl-ester

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
\paragraph{Why the collapse is structural.} For each cure, $E_{\max}=\sum_c m_c\eta_c$ with
the reversible/dormant classes near-saturated; the only term with headroom is the lost class.
Formally $\partial E_{\max}/\partial\eta_{\text{lost}}=m_{\text{lost}}>0$ dominates while the
other partials sit at their ceilings --- so $\eta_{\text{lost}}$ is the unique active
constraint in every domain. The convergence is therefore not coincidence but a property of
any cure whose disease leaves an irrecoverable-by-reactivation tissue fraction.
\paragraph{Why senolytic is the cross-cutting lever, not the regeneration arm.} Each domain
has a \emph{different} regeneration arm (Wnt/Turing follicle, BMP/Wnt bone, chondral, glial)
--- those are not shared. What \emph{is} shared is the upstream suppressor: senescent niche
cells gate all of them via the SASP \citep{moiseeva2023}. Removing the suppressor is the one
intervention that raises $\eta_{\text{lost}}$ regardless of which regeneration arm follows.
This is why a single senolytic, not a single regenerator, is the universal key.
\paragraph{One drug, four cures --- the economics.} A program that de-risks one molecule
(SENOLYX) against a bottleneck shared by four indications amortises discovery cost across all
four, each combined with its (cheaper, known-biology) regeneration arm. The negative-result
companion paper (AGA-CURE axis-collapse) and this positive structural result together argue
for re-ordering regenerative-medicine portfolios around the shared bottleneck.
\paragraph{Falsifiability.} The central claim fails if any chronic-degenerative cure with a
lost-tissue fraction does \emph{not} block on neogenesis, or if senolytic clearance does not
raise neogenesis efficiency in vitro. Both are directly testable (organoid + senescent
co-culture). The steric-gating sub-model was already falsified and discarded.

\paragraph{Combination regimen and sequencing.} Because senolytic clearance \emph{enables}
rather than \emph{performs} regeneration, the temporal order matters: the senescent niche must
be cleared \emph{before or alongside} the regeneration arm, not after, so that the cleared
window coincides with active morphogenesis. From the AGA arm-timing analysis, the durability
of restoration is a saturating function of when a permanence step is applied, with a knee
around the time gains are realised; the analogous prescription here is to dose SENOLYX as an
induction phase (clear the niche, $\phi\!\geq\!\phi^\ast$), run the domain-specific
regeneration arm during the cleared window, and only then apply any permanence/lock step. A
senolytic given after fibrosis has re-consolidated, or a regenerator given into an
un-cleared (SASP-suppressed) niche, both waste the intervention --- a prediction that the
combination must be \emph{sequenced}, not merely co-administered. This converts the four
single-disease regimens into one shared induction (SENOLYX) plus four short regeneration
tails, the practical form of the ``one drug, four cures'' economics.

\paragraph{Scope of generalisation.} The four cures here all share a non-scarring-to-fibrotic
gradient with a preserved dormant stem compartment. The result should extend to other
degenerations of this class (e.g. dermal, tendon, intervertebral disc) but \emph{not} to
diseases lacking a dormant reservoir or whose lost tissue is non-regenerable in principle ---
those would show no headroom even at $\phi=1$. The model makes that boundary explicit: if
$\sum_c m_c\eta_c^{\max}<0.90$ even at $\eta_{\text{lost}}=1$, no senolytic dose closes the
gate, and the disease falls outside the cross-cutting claim.

\paragraph{Proposed validation experiments (pre-registered predictions).} Three in-vitro
measurements would confirm or refute the core claims, each with a quantitative prediction:
(1) \emph{Senescence$\to$neogenesis co-culture.} Seed a hair-follicle (or periodontal/chondral/
retinal) organoid with a graded burden of senescent fibroblasts and measure de-novo unit
formation. Prediction: neogenesis efficiency falls monotonically with senescent burden and
recovers on senolytic clearance, crossing the $\eta_{\text{lost}}\!\approx\!0.84$ needed for
the cure gate at $\phi\!\approx\!60$--$80\%$ clearance. (2) \emph{Platelet-sparing assay.}
Compare navitoclax vs the CRBN-PROTAC on senescent-cell kill and platelet viability.
Prediction: comparable senolytic potency, ${\sim}20\times$ wider platelet-sparing window
(platelets CRBN-low). (3) \emph{$\beta$-gal cleavage selectivity.} Measure prodrug$\to$active
conversion in SA-$\beta$-gal-high (senescent) vs normal cells. Prediction: $5$--$50\times$
higher active-drug release in senescent cells, with binding affinity unchanged by the cap
(consistent with the falsified steric model). A negative on (1) would refute the universal
cross-cutting key; negatives on (2)/(3) would down-select the two selectivity routes but
leave the structural bottleneck result intact.

\section{Limitations and honest caveats}
\label{sec:limits}
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.2em
\item \textbf{No efficacy claim.} The result is structural (which axis is live), not a
demonstration that any agent cures any disease.
\item \textbf{Literature-order efficiencies.} Per-class $\eta$ and the $\phi\to\eta$ coupling
are literature-order (\tierOrange); the \emph{convergence} (same binding axis) is robust to
their exact values, the absolute ceilings are estimates.
\item \textbf{MM-GBSA magnitude.} Single-snapshot GBSA on a charged ligand corroborates sign
only; quantitative $\Delta G$ needs FEP.
\item \textbf{In-vitro gates.} Senescent-clearance$\to\eta_{\text{neo}}$ recovery, platelet
sparing, and $\beta$-gal cleavage selectivity are organoid/co-culture measurements, not closed here.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
Four unrelated chronic-degenerative cures are, in silico, the same problem wearing different
tissue: each blocks on lost-tissue neogenesis, and each is unblocked by clearing the
senescent niche that suppresses it. The most consequential next step is therefore not a
fourth disease program but a single, well-powered measurement --- does senolytic niche
clearance raise human neogenesis efficiency toward the $\sim 0.84$ the gate requires?

\section{Generic axis-collapse model (reproducibility listing)}
\label{app:code}
The entire cross-domain result is produced by one function applied to four manifests (single
dispatch; no per-disease code). Verbatim core:

\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\ttfamily\footnotesize,frame=single,xleftmargin=0pt,breaklines=true]
def axis_collapse(mass, eta, gate=0.90):
    # mass, eta: dict over classes {reversible, dormant, lost}
    ceiling = sum(mass[c] * eta[c] for c in mass)
    binding = min(mass, key=lambda c: eta[c])     # lowest-eta class = bottleneck
    return ceiling, binding, (ceiling >= gate)

def senolytic_lift(mass, eta, phi, gate=0.90):
    # phi = niche senescent-cell clearance fraction in [0,1]
    e = dict(eta)
    e['lost'] = eta['lost'] + phi*(1 - eta['lost'])  # clearance lifts neogenesis efficiency
    return axis_collapse(mass, e, gate)
