## Axis-by-axis re-confirmation

### Q1 — Direct collision: a small molecule that binds Cx32 to refold/re-traffic a CMTX1 mutant? → **NONE (re-confirmed)**
The CMTX1 competitor landscape is **entirely non-direct-small-molecule**, exactly as v1 stated:
- **Gene replacement (incumbent, dominant):** AAVrh10-hMPZ.GJB1 / AAV9 Schwann-cell-targeted GJB1 (Kleopa team), positive mouse data through 2025 — `cell.com/molecular-therapy-family/advances S2329-0501(23)00113-4` (PMC10460951); Institut de Myologie news Apr-2025. **CLOSED/competitor = gene therapy.**
- **HSP90 C-terminal modulator / cemdomespib (KU-596 novologue):** improves CMTX1 motor function via **Hsp70 induction, does NOT bind Cx32** — PMC9926526 (and ACS Pharmacol Transl Sci 2c00223). Indirect; mutation-agnostic. **OPEN line preserved (not a direct chaperone).**
- **Anti-Cx32 antibody abEC1.1-hIgG1:** biologic that *blocks* hemichannel dysfunction (extracellular domain), not a fold corrector — PMC11619691, doi:10.1186/s12964-024-01969-0. **OPEN line preserved (different modality).**
- No 2026 paper or patent on a Cx32-binding small-molecule corrector surfaced (patent search returned only unrelated lysosomal-storage chaperone patents). **NOT-FOUND.**

### Q2 — L143P (or any CMTX1) cryptic pocket; any connexin TM1/TM4 druggable cavity? → **NOT-FOUND (re-confirmed)**
- No literature/PDB describes an **L143P- (or any CMTX1-mutation-) induced cryptic pocket** in Cx32, nor a **TM1/TM4 inter-helical druggable cavity** in any connexin. **OPEN/novel.**
- **Adjacent (bounds the claim, does not collide):** *Lipid dependence of connexin-32 gap junction channel conformations*, Nat Commun 2025 (bioRxiv 2025.03.25.645310) describes a **WT N-terminal hydrophobic sterol/lipid pocket** in Cx32 that the **W3S** mutant *disrupts* — opposite logic (mutation *destroys* a WT pocket) and a different locus (N-terminus, not TM1/TM4). PDB 9QN9/9QNF.
- **Note (d6):** the β-connexin literature describes a generic WT "intracellular pocket lined by all four TM helices" (Springer Biol Res 2024, PMC11112876). This is a *WT constitutive* pocket discussed for charge-reversal modulation — **not** a mutation-induced cavity and **not** framed as a druggable TM1/TM4 fold-rescue site; it is adjacent context, not a collision, but should be cited so the TM1/TM4 framing is not overclaimed as "first to note any TM-helix pocket in a connexin."

### Q3 — Mutation-selective chaperone *concept* on connexins? → **PUBLISHED for Cx26 (the decisive prior art; re-verified by direct fetch)**
Fetched `PMC12504493` (Front Med 2025, VRT534@Cx26) and confirmed verbatim its scope:
- **Cx26 / hearing loss ONLY — Cx32 and CMT1X are never mentioned.**
- Binding site is **"near the pore region… not adjacent to the mutated residues, suggesting an allosteric mechanism"** — i.e. **near-pore allosteric, explicitly NOT a TM1/TM4 inter-helical interface.**
- **Does NOT use "cryptic pocket" / defined-volume druggability framing** (only docking kcal/mol).
- DOES report mutants gain **"multiple high-affinity (≤ −6 kcal/mol) binding positions"** vs weak WT binding — this is the shared mechanism (mutation increases mutant-selective binding) that makes our *concept* non-novel.
- Companion: *A Chemical Chaperone Restores Connexin 26 Mutant Activity*, ACS Pharmacol Transl Sci 2023, doi:10.1021/acsptsci.3c00056 (PMID 37470015) — MST shows higher affinity to mutant (Cx26K188N EC50 ~5 µM) than WT (~19 µM).
- **CLOSED line:** "first mutation-selective chemical chaperone for *a* connexin" and "first to show a connexin mutant gains/exposes a higher-affinity site" — **both owned by Cx26/VRT-534, must be cited.** **OPEN line preserved:** the *site type* (TM1/TM4 cryptic cavity, defined-volume framing) and the *indication* (Cx32/CMTX1) are not in that work.

### Q4 — 4-PBA / 2-naphthoate / aromatic chaperones on Cx32? → **PARTIAL (re-confirmed)**
- **2-naphthoate on any connexin:** NOT-FOUND.
- **4-PBA on Cx32 specifically:** NOT-FOUND. 4-PBA on other connexins is a **negative** precedent (4-PBA/TUDCA/glycerol failed to rescue GJB-family/EKVP mutant trafficking — Front Cell Dev Biol 2023, doi:10.3389/fcell.2023.1073805). Keep 4-PBA strictly as a **method anchor**, not a hit. **CLOSED caveat retained.**

### Q5 — L143P biology → **PUBLISHED (the intended anchor; unchanged)**
L143P = well-characterized CMT1X **TM3** LOF, Golgi-retained, no WT interaction — Vavlitou 2017 (PMID 28334782, doi:10.1093/hmg/ddx064); classic trafficking work Deschênes/Fischbeck J Neurosci 1997 (PMID 9364054). **d6 caveat retained:** L143 is a **TM3** residue; the *consequent* cavity is reported at TM1/TM4 — do not imply L143 sits in TM1/TM4.
